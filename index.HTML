About Dussehra

Dussehra, also known as Vijayadashami, is a vibrant and widely celebrated festival in India, symbolizing the victory of good over evil. Rooted in Hindu mythology, it marks Lord Rama's triumph over the demon king Ravana, and also honors Goddess Durga's victory over the buffalo demon Mahishasura. Celebrated with grandeur and enthusiasm, Dussehra features processions, cultural performances, and the burning of effigies of Ravana, signifying the destruction of evil. It is a time of joy, reflection, and community gatherings, where people come together to celebrate the triumph of righteousness and the renewal of hope and harmony.

Ram-Leela

Join us for the grand open-air play, Ram-Leela, where actors bring to life the ancient epic, the Ramayana. Watch the heroic journey of Lord Ram as he battles evil, saves the day, and brings righteousness back to the world. The dramatic performances and vivid storytelling will leave you spellbound.

03

Ravana Dahan

End the festivities with the spectacular Ravana Dahan, the symbolic burning of Ravana’s effigy. This is the dramatic climax of Dussehra and symbolizes the victory of good over evil. The towering effigy will go up in flames, accompanied by fireworks that light up the night sky.
